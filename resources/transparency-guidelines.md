# Transparency Guidelines

## Building Trust Through Radical Openness

Transparency is the foundation of accountability, the antidote to corruption, and the basis for public trust. These guidelines operationalize transparency in all Constitutional Forge work.

### Trust Building
- Citizens trust what they can see and verify
- Openness demonstrates good faith
- Visibility enables accountability
- Transparency builds legitimacy

### Corruption Prevention
- Sunlight is the best disinfectant
- Hidden processes invite abuse
- Transparency raises the cost of corruption
- Openness enables early detection

- Openness ensures equal access to knowledge
- Visibility supports meaningful engagement

### Learning and Improvement
- Open processes enable learning from experience
- Transparency allows replication of successes
- Visibility supports continuous improvement
- Openness advances collective knowledge

## üìú Transparency Principles

### 1. **Default to Open**

**Principle**: Openness is the default; secrecy requires justification

**In Practice**:
- All documents public unless specific reason for privacy
- All meetings open unless compelling confidentiality need
- All data accessible unless privacy/security concerns
- All decisions explained publicly

**Exceptions** (must be explicitly justified):
- Personal privacy protection
- Security concerns
- Ongoing law enforcement
- Competitive procurement
- Genuine trade secrets
- Attorney-client privilege

---

### 2. **Proactive Disclosure**

**Principle**: Share information without waiting to be asked

**In Practice**:
- Publish documents as created, not when requested
- Make data available in usable formats
- Provide context and explanation with raw data
- Update information regularly

**What to Proactively Disclose**:
- Decision-making processes and criteria
- Meeting agendas, materials, and minutes
- Budgets and financial information
- Contracts and agreements
- Performance data and metrics
- Evaluation results

---

### 3. **Accessible and Understandable**

**Principle**: Information must be genuinely accessible to be transparent

**In Practice**:
- Plain language, not jargon
- Multiple formats (text, visual, audio, video)
- Translated into relevant languages
- Available on accessible platforms
- Organized logically and searchably
- Summarized as well as detailed

**Accessibility Checklist**:
- [ ] Written in clear, plain language
- [ ] Key points highlighted or summarized
- [ ] Visual aids where helpful
- [ ] Available in needed languages
- [ ] Accessible to people with disabilities
- [ ] Easy to find and navigate

---

### 4. **Timely Publication**

**Principle**: Information should be available when it's useful

**In Practice**:
- Real-time or near-real-time when possible
- Before decisions are final, to enable input
- Regular scheduled updates
- Prompt response to information requests

**Timing Guidelines**:
- **Meeting materials**: Available at least 48 hours before
- **Draft proposals**: Published for comment period
- **Decisions**: Explained within 24 hours of making
- **Data**: Updated on regular schedule
- **Requests**: Responded to within 5 business days

---

### 5. **Complete and Accurate**

**Principle**: Transparency requires truth, not just disclosure

**In Practice**:
- Full context provided, not cherry-picked facts
- Uncertainties and limitations acknowledged
- Corrections made promptly when errors found
- Sources cited and verifiable
- Assumptions and reasoning explained

**Completeness Standards**:
- Background context included
- Alternative views noted
- Dissenting opinions documented
- Assumptions made explicit
- Evidence sources cited
- Limitations acknowledged

## üìä What to Make Transparent

### Decision-Making Processes

**Process Documentation**:
- Who makes what decisions and how
- Criteria used for decisions
- Steps in the decision process
- Timeline and milestones
- Opportunities for input

**Decision Records**:
- What was decided and when
- Who made the decision
- What alternatives were considered
- Rationale for the choice made
- Evidence and analysis used
- Dissenting views
- Implementation plan

**Example Template**:
```markdown
**Decision**: [What was decided]
**Date**: [When]
**Decision-Makers**: [Who decided]
**Process**: [How decision was made]
**Alternatives Considered**: [What else was considered]
**Rationale**: [Why this choice]
**Evidence**: [What information supported decision]
**Dissent**: [Any disagreements]
**Implementation**: [How it will be carried out]
```

---

### Financial Information

**Budget Transparency**:
- Proposed budgets with line-item detail
- Approved budgets with explanations
- Regular expenditure reports
- Variance analysis and explanations
- Multi-year financial projections

**Transaction Transparency**:
- All expenditures over threshold (e.g., $500)
- Payee, amount, purpose, date
- Contract details and terms
- Competitive processes used
- Relationships disclosed

**Revenue Transparency**:
- All income sources
- Amounts and terms
- Donor identities (unless legally prohibited)
- In-kind contributions
- Conflicts of interest

**Example Format**:
```markdown
**Budget Category**: [Category name]
**Budgeted**: $[Amount]
**Spent**: $[Amount]
**Variance**: [+/- $Amount and %]
**Explanation**: [Why different from budget]
**Major Expenditures**:
- [Date] - [Payee] - $[Amount] - [Purpose]
```

---

### Conflicts of Interest

**What to Disclose**:
- Financial interests in affected matters
- Personal relationships with interested parties
- Employment or business relationships
- Gifts or benefits received
- Future employment or opportunities
- Ideological commitments relevant to issue

**Disclosure Format**:
```markdown
**Individual**: [Name and role]
**Matter**: [What decision or issue]
**Potential Conflict**: [Nature of conflict]
**Details**: [Specific information]
**Mitigation**: [How conflict is managed]
**Date Disclosed**: [When]
```

**Management Options**:
- Full disclosure to public
- Recusal from decision-making
- Independent review
- Firewall procedures
- Divestiture if appropriate

---

### Stakeholder Input

**Consultation Transparency**:
- Who was consulted and when
- What they said (with permission)
- How input influenced decisions
- Why some input wasn't adopted
- Ongoing engagement opportunities

**Public Comment**:
- All public comments received
- Organized and searchable
- Response to common themes
- Individual responses when promised
- Summary of how input used

---

### Performance and Outcomes

**Performance Data**:
- Metrics tracked
- Current performance levels
- Trends over time
- Comparison to goals
- Comparison to benchmarks

**Evaluation Results**:
- Evaluation methods used
- Full findings, positive and negative
- Limitations of evaluation
- Recommendations and responses
- Follow-up action plans

**Impact Assessment**:
- Intended and unintended effects
- Distribution of benefits and burdens
- Equity analysis
- Long-term sustainability
- Lessons learned

---

### Meeting Records

**Before Meetings**:
- Notice of meeting (time, place, agenda)
- Background materials
- Proposals to be considered
- Public comment opportunities

**During Meetings**:
- Open to public unless justified exception
- Live streaming when feasible
- Recording for later access

**After Meetings**:
- Minutes with key discussion points
- Decisions made and votes taken
- Action items and follow-up
- Recording or transcript
- Supporting materials

### Documentation Standards

**Standard Template**:
Every document should include:
- Title and document type
- Date created and last updated
- Version number
- Author(s)
- Purpose
- Status (draft/final)
- Related documents
- Contact for questions

**Metadata**:
- Tags for searchability
- Category classification
- Date range or time period
- Geographic scope
- Stakeholders affected
- Keywords

**Version Control**:
- Track all changes
- Note who changed what when
- Explain reason for changes
- Archive previous versions
- Make version history available

## ‚öñÔ∏è Balancing Transparency and Privacy

### Personal Privacy Protection

**What to Protect**:
- Personal identifying information
- Medical information
- Financial details of individuals
- Home addresses and contact info
- Children's information
- Sensitive personal circumstances

**How to Protect**:
- Redact personal information
- Aggregate data to prevent identification
- Get consent before sharing personal stories
- Use pseudonyms when appropriate
- Separate personal from organizational

---

### Security Considerations

**Legitimate Secrecy**:
- Information that could enable crimes
- Security vulnerabilities
- Ongoing investigations
- Confidential informant protection
- Cybersecurity details

**Time-Limited Secrecy**:
- Reveal after risk period passes
- Explain why secret and when will be open
- Regular review of need for secrecy
- Default to disclosure when safe

---

### Strategic Considerations

**Competitive Procurement**:
- Proposals kept confidential during evaluation
- Disclosure after award
- Criteria and process public
- Evaluation summary public
- Full transparency post-award

**Negotiations**:
- Positions kept confidential during negotiation
- Final agreements fully public
- Public informed of process and timeline
- Option to input on parameters

**Deliberations**:
- Draft thinking can be private
- Final decisions fully explained
- Option for transparency even in drafting
- Clear when deliberation vs. decision

## üö® Red Flags: Transparency Failures

Watch for these warning signs:

### Process Red Flags
- [ ] Important decisions made without explanation
- [ ] Meetings held without public notice
- [ ] Documents "lost" or unavailable
- [ ] Long delays in responding to requests
- [ ] Excessive claims of exemptions

### Content Red Flags
- [ ] Redactions that seem excessive
- [ ] Vague or evasive explanations
- [ ] Missing financial details
- [ ] Conflicts of interest not disclosed
- [ ] Data presented without context

### Cultural Red Flags
- [ ] Hostile response to information requests
- [ ] Culture of secrecy and compartmentalization
- [ ] Lack of proactive disclosure
- [ ] No designated transparency responsibility
- [ ] Transparency seen as burden, not value
